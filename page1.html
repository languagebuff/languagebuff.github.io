Frequently Asked Questions

If you have a question that is not answered below, please contact us. We are an honest, family-owned business and will gladly take time to answer any questions that you might have about our company and our services.

typ

 Where are you located?

We are located in Tulsa, Oklahoma at 1507 E. 71st St. on the north side of the street, between the Farmer's Insurance office building and RiverGate Church. Our building is hard to miss. Just look for the building with the large bronze horse in front.

 Who conducts your auctions?

Daniel Glossop is our primary auctioneer, our company treasurer, and one of the original members of the founding family. He is a respected member of the Tulsa community and a recognized figure in the Oklahoma auction community. Our company regularly employs other experienced auctioneers who help relieve Dan during the course of an auction, which may run from 4 to 6 hours or more. Real estate auctions are conducted by licensed sales associates with Smith and Smith Realtors (Jim Smith, Owner/Broker).

 What does it cost to attend one of your auctions?

Absolutely nothing! There are no charges to attend our sale. You can come to bid and buy, or you can come to watch Dan Glossop and our other auctioneers in action.

  Do you charge a buyer's premium at your auctions? / What is a buyer's premium?

Yes. Currently, our buyer's premium is generally 10%. A buyer's premium is a charge attached to every purchase made at public auction. Auction houses are an expensive business with substantial overhead, and the buyer's premium helps us pay for labor, advertising, fuel, house expenses, etc. Large auction houses charge anywhere from 15%-35% or more; therefore, 10% is a very low buyer's premium.

 Do you accept absentee bids?

We do accept absentee bids on certain items. You must secure your absentee bid with Visa or Mastercard information. Please call us at (918) 494-5790 or (888) 222-6354 for more details about placing absentee bids.

 What forms of payment do you accept?

We accept cash, personal checks, cashier's checks, money orders, wire transfers and most major credit cards, including Visa, Mastercard, American Express and Discover.

 Do you accept returns on items? 

No. All items sold at auction are sold in "as is" condition, and all sales are final and non-negotiable. It is the customer's responsibility to inspect items and determine values. Once the auctioneer's gavel falls and the word "sold" is uttered, the item is the buyer’s.

 What does it cost to sell items at auction?

Items sold at auction are sold as consignments. Our company operates on a commission basis. This means that we will take a small portion of the final sale price for all consigned items sold at public auction (including online sales). For information on our commission rate, see here.

 What types of items can you sell?

Our company sells everything from the ordinary to the extraordinary! We are also licensed to sell real estate via our broker, Smith and Smith Realtors, and Firearms (we hold an FFL Class 01 license). If you wish to consign items for sale at our auctions, please call in advance. 

 Do you accept reserves on consigned items?

If you have an item of significant value consigned to sell at auction and wish to discuss the option of placing a reserve price on the item, please call us.

 How do you determine values and starting bids?

After 30+ years in this business, the auctioneer can usually estimate the average sale price of the items we sell. Dan's opening bids are based on past sales; however, when all is said and done, the bidders determine the value. As a courtesy to our customers, we start the bidding high and work our way down until someone likes the price.

 Do you advertise your auctions?

Yes! Our primary means of advertising is through ads in newspapers and other publications, Facebook, our e-mail list, and our website. Occasionally, our company will advertise sales through local radio and television stations. Our e-mail list covers several thousand repeat customers nationally and internationally. E-mail advertisements are sent out at least once a week.

 Do you offer safe and secure storage prior to your auctions?

Yes! Our company is fully licensed and insured. Our auction house is also equipped with a sophisticated anti-theft system, including indoor and outdoor surveillance, and several commercial grade safes. Consignments of jewelry, coins, and other fine valuables are usually relocated to our bank's vaults prior to the date of sale. If needed, we can make arrangements for additional security staff at our auction house or offsite.
