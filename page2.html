Southside Auction Co., Inc. was founded in 1967 by the Roach Family of Tulsa, Oklahoma. Gary Pruitt and Dan Glossop are still maintaining the family business with the help of Bud’s daughter Katie Roach, Claire Glossop (Dan's wife), David Hurst, and Robert "Bob" Black. We're a small family business, but we're proud of that.


The old brick building at 1507 E. 71st St. that houses Southside Auction Co., Inc. was the first structure in northeast Oklahoma built specifically for holding public auctions. The building was built in 1956 by the late auctioneer, Lloyd L. Kraus. Mr. Kraus was a cattle auctioneer from Welch, Oklahoma who began his auctioneering career in the 1930s. After moving to Tulsa in 1946, Mr. Kraus quickly established the Kraus Auction Co. at 45th St. and Peoria Ave. In order to accommodate the large crowds that began attending the Kraus public auctions, Mr. Kraus decided to purchase a few acres of land in rural south Tulsa and construct a building to meet the needs of his growing business. The Kraus Auction Co. was a success and held regular public auctions every Monday and Thursday nights.

After the death of Lloyd L. Kraus, Paul Roach Sr. purchased the Kraus Auction Co. building and land from Mrs. Kraus. In 1967, Paul Roach Sr. established Southside Auction Co., Inc. with the help of his wife, Hazel and his two children, Paul "Bud" Roach Jr., and Gail.

Since the establishment of Southside Auction Co., Inc. in 1967, Bud Roach became the primary auctioneer. Since Bud's passing in 2017, his nephew Dan has taken over the reigns as Auctioneer.

Paul James Roach, Jr., known to many as “Bud,” passed away on the evening of November 7th, 2017 in Tulsa, Oklahoma. Bud was well known as the founder of Southside Auction Co., Inc. in Tulsa, Oklahoma, and its primary auctioneer since its establishment in 1967. For almost 50 years, Bud conducted weekly public auctions in Tulsa and the surrounding area. Everyone new Bud as a fun-loving guy with a big heart. His love for all things related to the Old West and his big moustache were two enduring and unforgettable aspects of his persona. Although Bud may now be gone, he will never be forgotten.
